About The Location

Kinabalu National Park: As Malaysia's first UNESCO World Heritage Site and part of the Kinabalu UNESCO Global Geopark, this park boasts incredible biodiversity. Covering 754 square kilometres at 1,500 metres above sea level, it hosts 5,000-6,000 vascular plant species, many of which are endemic. Recognized as a Centre of Plant Diversity for Southeast Asia, the park features the majestic Mount Kinabalu, standing at 4,095.2 meters. Visitors can enjoy lush montane forests, a botanic garden with endemic species, and nature trails suitable for both leisurely strolls and adventurous hikes.

Desa Cow Farm: Nicknamed the Little New Zealand of Sabah, this 199-hectare farm houses Friesian cows that produce nearly a million liters of fresh milk annually. Visitors can learn about milk processing, enjoy fresh yoghurt, and bottle-feed adorable calves. With Mount Kinabalu as a stunning backdrop, don't miss the chance to capture some unforgettable photos.

North Borneo Cruises: Embark on the Sunset Dinner Cruise for an unforgettable evening on the South China Sea. Explore the vessel, take in panoramic ocean views from the upper deck, and enjoy the warmth of the setting sun and the sea breeze. Savor a delicious buffet with local and international dishes, and enjoy dancing and singing with the live band on stage before you depart.

Nabalu: A favored stop for travelers en route to Kundasang. Visit the local market for souvenirs and produce, and don't miss the viewing point offering stunning vistas of Mount Kinabalu.
